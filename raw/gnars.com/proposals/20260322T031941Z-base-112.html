Description

TL;DR

Game of Pam Gnars 2026 is an initiative conceived by Petterson Gomes, known in the skateboarding community as Pamtech, a professional skateboarder since the 2000s and founder of the Game of Pam movement. The project is developed in partnership with Gnars DAO and proposes an event that connects skateboarding, urban culture, social impact, and emerging technologies.

Using skateboarding as a tool for social and urban transformation, Game of Pam promotes inclusion between skateboarders and the broader community, encourages sustainable practices, and experiments with Web3 tools as enablers of impact, transparency, and decentralization.

Project Concept

Game of Pam Gnars 2026 is a multidimensional event that integrates sport, culture, education, and social innovation within an accessible public space. The proposal goes beyond competition, creating an environment for exchange between athletes, artists, independent collectives, and the local community.

The initiative reinforces skateboarding as a cultural language and a community-building tool, fostering new talent, creative entrepreneurship, and the positive occupation of urban space.

Objectives

  • Encourage youth through sport and urban culture;
  • Promote the exchange of experiences among athletes, artists, and local communities;
  • Implement environmentally sustainable practices throughout the event;
  • Strengthen social inclusion initiatives with a focus on gender, race, age, and LGBTQIAPN+ diversity;
  • Build a collaborative network connecting collectives, social projects, and independent brands.

Institutional Benefits for Gnars DAO

  • Placement of the Gnars logo across all event promotional materials (digital and print), including banners, stickers, flyers, social media content, and on-chain digital assets;
  • Institutional mentions of Gnars during the opening and closing ceremonies;
  • Collaborative posts on the official Game of Pam and Gnars DAO channels, announcing the partnership and later sharing photo and video content from the event;
  • Direct connection with a diverse, creative, and culturally engaged audience composed of skateboarders, young creators, independent collectives, and creative economy agents;
  • Activation of Gnars’ presence in the physical world, introducing its values to participants who typically operate outside the Web3 environment.

This integration encourages participants to explore engagement beyond traditional Web2 platforms, while promoting transparency, collective participation, and sustainable community growth.

Game of Pam Background

Over the years, Game of Pam has toured several cities across Brazil, strengthening local skate scenes in a collaborative and independent way. Each edition contributes to local development by connecting skateboarders, independent brands, and grassroots cultural initiatives rooted in their communities.

Total Impact:

  • 2,000+ skaters participants
  • 500k+ views on social media content
  • 50+ independent brands supported
  • Zero corporate sponsorship - 100% independent

Event Activities

Competition

The main activity of the event will be a flatground SKATE-format championship, in which athletes are not allowed to place their feet on the ground during trick execution.

Main rules:

  • The first athlete to complete the word “SKATE” due to mistakes will be eliminated;
  • Prohibited tricks include handplants, bonelesses, and grabs;
  • The final letter allows two attempts;
  • On offense, if the heel or toes touch the ground, the trick must be repeated;
  • On defense, there is a greater margin for error, with the final decision made by the judge;
  • All tricks must be performed with the skateboard leaving the ground.

Why Game of Pam and Gnars DAO

Game of Pam and Gnars DAO share the same vision: using skateboarding as a tool for culture, community, and social impact. Game of Pam strengthens local scenes through an accessible and inclusive public-space event, encouraging new talent, knowledge exchange, and creative entrepreneurship.

Gnars DAO operates to protect, value, and expand skate culture in a decentralized manner, supporting authentic initiatives that emerge directly from the community.

This partnership represents a bridge between the physical and digital worlds, connecting the tradition of skateboarding with new models of collective organization, funding, and support enabled by Web3, while preserving the core values and identity of skate culture.

Target Audience

  • Young people and adults who practice skateboarding from different regions of Brazil;
  • Children and families interested in skate culture;
  • Micro-entrepreneurs, cultural collectives, and creative economy agents;
  • Educators, schools, and local social projects.
