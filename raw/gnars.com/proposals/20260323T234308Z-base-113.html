Summary

We are producing a 15–20 minute documentary capturing the culture, experimentation, and coordination emerging from communities operating on Nouns Builder during ETH Boulder and ETHDenver 2026.

This project is currently proposed to Nouns Builder DAO as a broader ecosystem production. That proposal establishes baseline funding. The final scale and polish of the film evolves based on co-sponsorship participation from featured communities.

Gnars DAO will be featured prominently in the documentary, including:

  • Coverage of Gnars community presence at ETHDenver
  • In-depth exploration of Gnars’ community creator coin experimentation
  • Illustration of pairing creator coins with the GNARS community token
  • Extended coverage of the Skate Across Africa project
  • Interviews with Gnars contributors and participants

We are requesting $750 USD from Gnars DAO as a co-sponsor to support:

  • Craft services
  • Production kit fees
  • Expanded post-production capacity

This same co-sponsorship structure is being offered to additional Builder-based communities featured in the film.

Larger Budget Context

The Nouns Builder proposal establishes baseline execution (~$3,000 USD equivalent).
Co-sponsorship contributions allow us to:

  • Expand interview depth

  • Strengthen post-production refinement
  • Enhance distribution and reuse

Gnars’ contribution directly strengthens the quality and depth of its representation within the film.

Why Gnars Is Central to This Story

Gnars is not just a participating DAO — it represents forward experimentation within the Builder ecosystem.

Specifically:

Community Creator Coin Innovation

Gnars’ model of pairing creator coins with the GNARS community token demonstrates:

  • Experimental token alignment
  • Incentivized cultural participation
  • Hybrid creator + DAO value loops
  • Near-future coining mechanics that Builder is evolving toward

This is a live example of token experimentation in motion — not theory.

Documenting it allows the film to illustrate what the protocol enables when culture and incentives intersect.

Skate Across Africa

The Skate Across Africa initiative is a powerful, real-world example of:

  • Athletic sponsorship through decentralized coordination
  • Community-funded cultural impact
  • Global public goods alignment
  • Onchain-backed cultural expansion

It embodies the type of sponsorship and athletic experimentation that Gnars uniquely facilitates.

This project visually and narratively anchors the film’s exploration of what decentralized communities can accomplish.
