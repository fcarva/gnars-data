Description

TL;DR

This proposal aims to preserve Bless Skateshop, a cornerstone of São Paulo and Brazil's skateboarding community, founded by skateboarders for skateboarders. Bless Skateshop plays a vital role in the local community, especially during the challenging times brought on by the COVID-19 pandemic. It serves as a guardian of Sopa de Letras Square, home to the only DIY skatepark fully supported by the Gnars community.

The recovery of Bless Skateshop is essential. With the invaluable support of skateboarder and artist Will Dias, a dedicated collaborator committed to our growth and progress, we can ensure the skateshop continues to thrive and support the skateboarding culture.

Introduction

  • We are Bless Skateshop, established in 2012 in the vibrant neighborhood of Santana, São Paulo. Since then, we have become a focal point for the local skateboarding community, dedicated to promoting and enhancing skateboarding culture in the region. Located next to the famous sopa de letras square, our store has become a meeting point for skaters, friends and customers, united by the same passion: skateboarding. We constantly seek to improve our space and boost skateboarding through community initiatives and events, always keeping our mission as a priority.

A brief story of what it was like to go to another country, Peru, to buy skateboard decks

For years, we've dreamed of having Bless skateboard decks, manufactured by an American company, reaching the quality of the Real, DGK and Baker brands. The scarcity of materials in Brazil has made this goal even more challenging.

At the end of 2021, an unexpected opportunity made this dream come true. After providing sales advice to my friend Julio De la Cruz, I received payment for the customized forms I had dreamed of. To get them, however, we had to travel to Peru.

A journey to Assis Brasil, with a tight deadline to pick up the shapes in Puerto Maldonado. The trip was full of anxiety and challenges, but determination drove me on.

The crossing to Peru was intense, and the lush jungle landscape made the adventure unique. Holding the shapes in Puerto Maldonado, I felt that all the effort had been worth it.

When I returned to Bless Skateshop, I brought back not only quality shapes, but a story of overcoming. This journey symbolizes perseverance for the skateboarding community in the northern zone of São Paulo, showing that we should never give up on our dreams. Skateboarding, more than a sport, is a way of life that teaches us to persist and overcome adversity.

To help Bless Skateshop emerge from bankruptcy, we have developed a strategic plan that includes the following key elements:

  • Financial Review: Carefully evaluate the store's finances to identify areas of waste, reduce unnecessary costs and optimize cash flow.

  • Product Diversification: Expand the mix of products on offer to attract a wider variety of customers. This can include accessories, clothing, footwear and even skateboard-related services such as lessons or events.

  • Marketing and Advertising: Invest in effective marketing strategies to increase the store's visibility, both locally and online. This can include social media campaigns, partnerships with influencers, promotional events and targeted ads.

  • E-commerce: Expand operations into e-commerce, updating the online store to reach a wider audience and facilitate sales beyond the store's physical reach.

  • Strategic Partnerships: Seek partnerships with local collaborators, and other businesses in the community to increase exposure and collaborate on joint initiatives.

  • Long-Term Financial Planning: Develop a sustainable long-term financial plan with clear goals and strategies to ensure the store's financial stability in the future. By implementing these strategies consistently and adapting to the needs of the market and the community, Bless Skateshop can work to overcome bankruptcy and thrive again.

Objective of the Bless x GNARS Plan:
